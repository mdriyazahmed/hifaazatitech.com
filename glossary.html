Cybersecurity Glossary

Comprehensive, up-to-date definitions of essential cybersecurity terms and acronyms.

Browse Terms

Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)
Prolonged, targeted intrusion where an attacker maintains stealthy access to exfiltrate data.
Authentication
Verifying identity of a user, device, or process before granting access.
Botnet
Network of compromised devices remotely controlled to launch coordinated attacks.
Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
Executive responsible for cybersecurity strategy, risk management, and governance.
Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)
Overwhelming a service with traffic from many sources to render it unavailable.
Encryption
Transforming data into unreadable form to protect confidentiality and integrity.
Firewall
Security control that filters network traffic based on defined policies.
Honeypot
Decoy system designed to lure and study attackers and techniques.
Incident Response
Preparation, detection, containment, eradication, recovery, and lessons learned.
Malware
Malicious software such as viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, and ransomware.
Penetration Testing
Simulated attack to identify exploitable vulnerabilities and validate defenses.
Phishing
Deceptive communication to trick recipients into revealing sensitive information.
Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)
Aggregates, normalizes, correlates, and analyzes logs for detection and compliance.
Security Operations Center (SOC)
Team that monitors, detects, investigates, and responds to cyber threats.
Vulnerability
Weakness that can be exploited to impact confidentiality, integrity, or availability.
Zero-day
Previously unknown vulnerability with no available patch at time of exploitation.
